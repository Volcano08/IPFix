How to Fix an Expired IP Address

By Patrick Hendrix

An expired IP Address can lead to a multitude of issues, such as loss of connectivity, an inability to access network resources, IP address conflicts, general security risks and much more. Luckily, this article is here to help you resolve your issues.

Solution 1: Release or Renew the IP Address

To release and renew the IP address in Linux:

  1. Click on the network icon in the top-right corner of the screen.
  2. Select "Settings".
  3. In the settings window, click on "Network".
  4. Choose the network connection you want to configure (e.g., Wi-Fi or Wired).
  5. Click on the gear icon next to the connection name.
  6. Under IPv4, choose "Automatic (DHCP)" from the dropdown menu.
  7. Finally, click "Apply" to save the settings.

Solution 2: Assign a Static IP Address

To assign a static IP address in Linux:

  1. Click on the network icon in the top-right corner of the screen.
  2. Select "Settings".
  3. In the settings window, click on "Network".
  4. Choose the network connection you want to configure (e.g., Wi-Fi or Wired).
  5. Click on the gear icon next to the connection name.
  6. Under IPv4, choose "Manual" from the dropdown menu.
  7. Add the desired IP address, subnet mask, gateway, and DNS servers.
  8. Click "Apply" to save the settings.
